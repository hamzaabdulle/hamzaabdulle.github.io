About Me

Hello! I’m Hamza Abdulle, an aspiring software developer with a strong background in architectural technology. I am currently an undergraduate student in Computer Science at Toronto Metropolitan University, driven by a passion for building innovative digital solutions and visually stunning architectural designs. My journey in the world of architecture began with a diploma in Architectural Technology from Sheridan College, where I honed my skills in 3D modeling, design principles, and project management. My architectural projects, which ranged from residential designs to commercial layouts, taught me the importance of precision and creativity. Seeking to merge my architectural expertise with cutting-edge technology, I ventured into the realm of computer science. This transition was fueled by my interest in how software development can enhance and innovate within architectural design and beyond. I am particularly drawn to web development and have undertaken several projects, including a web application that allows users to track jobs and tasks for project management. In both domains, I strive for excellence and innovation. My goal is to continue developing my skills in both architecture and technology, creating solutions that make a meaningful impact. When I'm not coding or drafting, you might find me exploring the latest in tech gadgets, engaging in sports, or experimenting with virtual photography. Thank you for visiting my portfolio. Please feel free to browse my projects and reach out if you’d like to discuss potential collaborations or opportunities.
